About The Hackathon

Ctrl + Space Hackathon brings together innovators, developers, engineers, and space enthusiasts to solve real-world challenges in space technology. Over 48 hours, teams will collaborate to create groundbreaking solutions that push the boundaries of what's possible in space exploration and technology.

Whether you're a seasoned developer, a space science expert, or simply passionate about the cosmos, this hackathon offers a unique opportunity to contribute to the future of space technology.

Hackathon Challenges

Satellite Data Analysis

Develop innovative solutions using satellite data to address environmental monitoring, disaster response, or urban planning challenges.

Space Robotics

Design autonomous robotic systems for space exploration, maintenance, or construction that can operate in extreme environments.

Asteroid Mining

Create solutions for identifying, reaching, and extracting valuable resources from asteroids to support space exploration and Earth's resource needs.

Life Support Systems

Develop innovative life support technologies for long-duration space missions, focusing on sustainability and resource efficiency.

Space Communications

Design next-generation communication systems for reliable data transmission across vast distances in space.

Launch Technologies

Innovate on rocket design, propulsion systems, or launch methodologies to reduce costs and increase accessibility to space.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate?

The hackathon is open to students, professionals, and space enthusiasts of all backgrounds. Participants must be 18 years or older. Teams can consist of 2-5 members.

Do I need to have a team before registering?

No, you can register individually and form teams during the team formation session on the first day of the hackathon. We'll have activities to help you find team members with complementary skills.

What should I bring?

Bring your laptop, charger, any hardware you plan to use for your project, and personal items for the duration of the event. Food and refreshments will be provided.

Is there a registration fee?

No, participation in the SpaceTech Hackathon is completely free, thanks to our generous sponsors.

What are the judging criteria?

Projects will be judged based on innovation, technical complexity, practicality, potential impact, and presentation quality. Detailed criteria will be shared during the opening ceremony.

Will there be mentors available?

Yes, industry experts and mentors will be available throughout the hackathon to provide guidance, technical support, and feedback on your projects.
